For TWS standard, latencies are measured from arrival of audio in the audio subsystem to the synchronised rendering by TWS master and TWS slave.

Macro Definition Documentation

◆ PCM_LATENCY_BUFFER_MS

#define PCM_LATENCY_BUFFER_MS   MAX(50, (TWS_STANDARD_LATENCY_MS - 50))

Buffering in the TWS standard graph is split between the (pre) source sync and pre-decoder buffers. The buffer after source sync is treated as "headroom", since it is comparatively small (4 * kick period). So for SBC/AAC, the entire TTP latency is split over these two buffers. AptX will have some extra buffer space since the decoding is split across two operators - there will be an extra buffer between the demux operator and aptX mono decoder.

◆ PRE_DECODER_BUFFER_MS

#define PRE_DECODER_BUFFER_MS   MAX(50, (TWS_STANDARD_LATENCY_MAX_MS - PCM_LATENCY_BUFFER_MS))

The difference between the maximum latency and the PCM buffer latency is the amount of buffering required pre-decoder. It must be possible for the buffer to accommodate the maximum allowed latency.
